# Dark Sun Setting Rules Spec

This file summarizes the setting-level rules that differ from standard D&D 2024 and should be preserved during ingestion.

## Core setting assumptions
- Standard Cleric is fully replaced by Elemental Cleric.
- Paladin is unavailable.
- Sorcerer is unavailable.
- Bard is allowed but requires a future Athasian replacement implementation.
- Psionicist requires a future implementation stub.
- Every character starts with one Wild Talent.
- Arcane casters must be Preservers or Defilers.
- Preserver rules are defined.
- Defiler rules are intentionally deferred.
- Resurrection is governed by the revised spell dataset, not by a hard global ban.
- The setting uses a five-tradition magic framework:
  - Arcane
  - Divine
  - Nature
  - Elemental
  - Psionics

## Subclass restrictions
Unavailable in Dark Sun:
- Barbarian: Path of the Zealot
- Druid: Circle of the Sea
- Fighter: Eldritch Knight
- Monk: Warrior of Shadow
- Ranger: Fey Wanderer
- Ranger: Gloom Stalker
- Rogue: Arcane Trickster
- Warlock: The Archfey

## Languages
- Characters know Common plus two additional languages.
- Language knowledge grants speech only, not literacy.
- Literacy may be granted by classes, feats, or other features.
- Dark Sun uses city languages and custom standard/exotic language pools.

## Skills
The setting adds these skills alongside existing PHB skills:
- Spellcraft (Arcane)
- Spellcraft (Divine)
- Spellcraft (Nature)
- Spellcraft (Elemental)
- Spellcraft (Psionics)
- Psionics

## Wild Talents
Every character begins with one Wild Talent from the provided table.

## Preservers
Preservers use:
- spell points
- reaction spell exhaustion
- one selected Preserver Casting penalty per spell
- expanded ritual casting
- group casting
- Rite of Blood tracking

Preserver mechanics should be preserved even if the engine cannot execute them fully yet.
